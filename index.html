AutoMID: Automated Molecular Identity Disambiguator

Project Status: This repository serves as a validated, longitudinal reference benchmark for chemical data integrity.

AutoMID is a systematic, hierarchical schema of key metadata features of small molecules, providing a comprehensive framework to ensure the application of FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) principles in scientific data management.

Project Purpose

In the era of AI-driven drug discovery, high-quality data is the primary bottleneck. AutoMID acts as a deterministic "chemical guardrail," ensuring that chemical entities utilized in biological assays are accurately represented and correctly disambiguated before downstream analysis.

Key Methodology: The Transformation Hierarchy

Our approach leverages a structured transformation hierarchy ($S_0 - S_8$):

  • Standardization (S0 - S3): Canonicalization, desalting, and charge neutralization.
  • Abstraction (S4 - S8): Progressive removal of structural complexity (tautomers, isotopes, stereochemistry, bond/atom connectivity) to uncover underlying structural identities and register errors.

Longitudinal Validation (Case Studies)

A 2026 retrospective audit of discrepancies identified in 2021 confirms that AutoMID pre-emptively identifies "structural identity crises" years before manual curation catches them.

Compound Type of Discrepancy 2026 Validation Status
JQ1 Stereochemistry ($S_5 \to S_6$) Resolved: Strictly defined as (+)-enantiomer.
